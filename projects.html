Projects

Surface station revisions

Redesigned the surface (control) station, which is a a collection of equipment organized into a box for portability that is used control the ROV. Originally, the surface station had a keyboard holder which meant there was a indent in the middle of the main platform. The router was also mounted onto the indented keyboard holder, which resulted in the ends of the nails sticking out of the wood and being a safety hazard. I proposed and made changes to the station such that it now has a flat surface to work on, with the router and many other devices mounted to the bottom of the case using velcro for safety.

Manipulator Idea Generation

Generated manipulator ideas to complete a specific task in the 2022 MATE competition. This task involved retireving a liquid sample, thus a manipulator that can move the piston of a syringe up and down to retrieve liquid was needed. I made a prototype for a manipulator that could achieve this mission. I was not able to finish prototyping before the competition, but this was a great way to learn how to apply my CAD knowledge.

Modular Buoyancy Device

Generated ideas for a modular ballasting system for the ROV. The ROV is a underwater robot. Since we use it in salt water pools to test and fresh water for the competition, we needed a modular ballastying system to quickly change between different density test fluids. My ideas were based on creating a modular weight attatchment in which we could easily add or remove weights to adjust buoyancy by simply "clicking in" an attatchment. My ideas were not selected in the final decision process, but seeing other people's ideas helped me learn more about underwater robotics and this project was a great experience overall.
